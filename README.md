# Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard

## Overview
This project presents an interactive Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard built using Microsoft Excel. It focuses on analyzing sales performance, customer behavior, and product trends to derive meaningful business insights.

The dashboard leverages advanced Excel features like Power Query, Power Pivot (Data Model), and DAX measures to transform raw data into a structured and insightful visualization.

## Objectives
- Analyze overall sales performance  
- Understand customer footfall and ordering patterns  
- Identify top-performing products  
- Evaluate category-wise contribution  
- Track trends across time (hours, weekdays, months)  

## Business Questions Answered
This dashboard helps answer key business questions such as:

- What is the total revenue generated by the coffee shop?  
- How many customers (footfall) visit the store?  
- What is the average bill value per customer?  
- How many orders does each customer place on average?  
- At what time of the day are sales highest?  
- Which days of the week generate the most orders?  
- Which store location performs best in terms of sales and footfall?  
- What is the distribution of product sizes?  
- Which product categories contribute the most to revenue?  
- What are the top-selling products?  

## Tools & Technologies
- Microsoft Excel  
- Power Query – Data cleaning, transformation, and loading  
- Power Pivot (Data Model) – Data modeling and relationships  
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – KPI calculations and measures  
- Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts – Data visualization  

## Key KPIs
- Total Sales: $6,98,812.33  
- Total Footfall: 149,116  
- Average Order per Person: 1.44  
- Average Bill per Person: 4.69  

## Dashboard Insights

### Sales Trend by Hour
- Peak sales occur during morning hours (8 AM – 10 AM)  
- Gradual decline observed post afternoon  

### Orders by Weekday
- Thursday and Friday have the highest order volume  
- Saturday shows the lowest activity  

### Store Location Analysis
- Hell’s Kitchen generates the highest revenue  
- Astoria shows moderate performance  
- Lower Manhattan has relatively lower sales  

### Size Distribution
- Large and Regular sizes dominate orders  
- Smaller sizes contribute less to total sales  

### Category Distribution
- Coffee is the leading category  
- Followed by bakery and other beverages  

### Top Products
- Barista Espresso is the top-selling product  
- Other strong performers include brewed coffee and hot chocolate  

## Data Processing Workflow
1. Data Extraction & Cleaning  
   - Imported raw datasets into Excel  
   - Cleaned and transformed data using Power Query  

2. Data Modeling  
   - Created relationships using Power Pivot (Data Model)  
   - Structured multiple tables for efficient analysis  

3. KPI Calculation  
   - Built DAX measures for:
     - Total Sales  
     - Average Bill per Person  
     - Average Orders per Person  
     - Total Footfall  

4. Visualization  
   - Designed interactive Pivot Charts and KPI cards  
   - Applied filters for months and weekdays
